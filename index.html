Our Story

From the Fish Farm to Digital Innovation

I was born and raised in a fish farming family in Brittany. As the daughter and sister of fish farmers, I’ve spent my life surrounded by ponds, trout, and the daily challenges of aquaculture. From a young age, I saw firsthand how time-consuming and error-prone manual record-keeping could be— whether it was tracking feedings, monitoring water quality, or managing stock levels.

After working in logistics and production, where I developed a deep understanding of quality requirements and traceability, I realized that the tools available to fish farmers were often outdated or ill-suited to their needs. Spreadsheets, paper logs, and whiteboards were the norm, but they came with risks: lost data, human errors, and inefficiencies that could impact both productivity and animal welfare.

Why Dluzh Breizh Edith?

Dluzh Breizh Edith was born from a simple idea: fish farmers deserve tools as dedicated as they are. I combined my on-the-ground experience in aquaculture with my passion for digital solutions to create an app that truly understands the needs of small and medium-sized farms.

With a background in logistics and production, I knew the importance of traceability, precision, and efficiency. But I also knew that most digital tools were either too complex or too generic for fish farmers. So, I built Dluzh Breizh Edith to be:

By Fish Farmers

Designed with the input of real fish farmers, including my family.

For Traceability

Built to meet the strictest quality and traceability standards.

With Passion

A tool created out of love for aquaculture and innovation.

A Tool for the Future

Today, Dluzh Breizh Edith helps fish farmers like my family save time, reduce errors, and focus on what matters most: raising healthy fish. By digitizing daily tasks—like feeding records, water quality checks, and stock management— we’re making it easier to meet quality standards and improve productivity.

This project is also my Portfolio Project for Holberton School, where I’ve honed my skills in Django, PostgreSQL, and web development to build tools that make a real difference. Because at the end of the day, Dluzh Breizh Edith isn’t just an app—it’s a bridge between tradition and innovation, and a testament to the hard work of fish farmers everywhere.
